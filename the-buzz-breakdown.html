The Buzz Breakdown

The science behind your energy

What is caffiene and where does it come from?

Caffeine is a natural stimulant found in coffee beans, tea leaves, and cacao pods. It’s the world’s most popular pick-me-up, boosting energy, focus, and alertness. People use it to wake up, stay sharp at work, or power through a workout.

How caffiene works

Caffeine blocks adenosine, a chemical that makes you feel sleepy. This allows dopamine and adrenaline to increase, making you feel more awake, alert, and even happier. That’s why caffeine is such an effective energy booster!

Absorption and half life

Caffeine kicks in within 30 to 60 minutes and lasts in your system for 3 to 5 hours, depending on your metabolism. That morning coffee can still be affecting you in the afternoon, so drinking caffeine too late may disrupt your sleep.

Effects on the brain

Caffeine improves focus by blocking sleep signals and increasing dopamine, the brain’s "feel-good" chemical. However, regular use can lead to dependence, and skipping caffeine may cause headaches, fatigue, or irritability.

Effects on the body

Caffeine raises heart rate and metabolism, making it common in weight-loss supplements. However, it can also cause jitters, dehydration, and in high doses, heart issues. Staying hydrated helps balance its effects.

Tolerance and withdrawl

Over time, your body builds tolerance, meaning you need more caffeine for the same effect. Cutting back suddenly can cause withdrawal symptoms like headaches and fatigue. To reset, take a short caffeine break or gradually reduce intake.
